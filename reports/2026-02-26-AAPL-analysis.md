# Apple Inc. (AAPL) Financial Analysis Report
**Date:** 2026-02-26

## 1. Market Events & News
The current market sentiment for Apple is mixed-positive. The stock recently saw a 2.2% increase to $272.14 on February 24, 2026, largely driven by a strong quarterly earnings report. Analysts currently hold a "Moderate Buy" consensus with an average price target of around $292.

Key factors influencing the stock include:
- **Positive Drivers**: A strategic shift to move some Mac Mini production to the U.S., continued strong growth in the Services segment, and anticipation for a product event on March 4, 2026, where the iPhone 17E and new MacBooks are expected to be unveiled.
- **Risks & Concerns**: Scrutiny over the company's supply chain entanglement with China continues, and some investors note that Apple's rollout of new AI features, particularly for Siri, is lagging behind competitors.

## 2. Earnings Analysis
Apple's recent financial performance has been robust, beating market expectations.

### Q1 FY2026 (Ended December 27, 2025)
- **EPS**: $2.84 (beating estimates of $2.67)
- **Revenue**: $143.8 billion (beating estimates of $138.25B), representing a 16-19% increase year-over-year.
- **Highlights**: Performance was driven by all-time revenue records in both the iPhone and Services segments.

### Q4 FY2025 (Ended September 27, 2025)
- **EPS**: $1.85 (adjusted), a 13% year-over-year increase.
- **Revenue**: $102.5 billion, an 8% year-over-year increase.
- **Segment Performance**:
    - **iPhone**: $49.0B (+6% Y/Y), driven by strong demand for the iPhone 17 series.
    - **Services**: $28.8B (+15% Y/Y), reaching an all-time record.
    - **Mac**: $8.7B (+13% Y/Y).

## 3. Data & Fundamentals
- **P/E Ratio**: Approximately 30-31x estimated FY2026 earnings, which is a high valuation relative to many peers.
- **Dividend Yield**: ~0.4%, based on an annualized dividend of $1.04 per share.
- **Revenue Growth**: Q1 2026 revenue grew 16% year-over-year.
- **Valuation**: While DCF models suggest an intrinsic value around $243, analyst consensus and other narratives place the fair value closer to $275-$288.

## 4. Key Earnings Drivers
The primary forces behind Apple's strong financial performance are:
- **iPhone Demand**: The iPhone remains the single most significant revenue source, with the latest models driving record sales across all geographic regions.
- **Services Growth**: The Services division (including the App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, etc.) continues its impressive growth trajectory, delivering high-margin, recurring revenue. The segment surpassed $100B in revenue for the fiscal year 2025.
- **Installed Base**: Apple's ecosystem is supported by a massive and loyal customer base, with over 2.5 billion active devices worldwide. This base provides a strong foundation for Services growth and future hardware sales.
- **Capital Returns**: The company continues to return significant value to shareholders, with $32 billion distributed through dividends and buybacks in Q1 2026 alone.

## 5. Recommendation
Based on the analysis, the consensus view is a **Moderate Buy**.

The company's strong brand, exceptional earnings performance, and massive cash flow provide a solid foundation. While the high P/E ratio and risks related to competition in AI and supply chain geopolitics are notable, the consistent growth in the high-margin Services segment and unwavering demand for the iPhone continue to support a positive outlook.
